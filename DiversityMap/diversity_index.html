How the Diversity Indicies Were Built

A diversity index measures the evenness of distribution within a population. The diversity indices here are based on Shannon entropy. Three indices were calculated based on data from the 2006-2010, 2007-2011, 2008-2012, 2009-2013, 2010-2014, 2011-2015, and 2012-2016 5-year estimates of the American Community Survey. One index (ethnoracial diversity) was additionally calculated from 1980, 1990, and 2000 US Census counts using NHGIS IPUMS data tables. The index values are between 0 and 1, the value 1 representing an absolutely even distribution among the categories, 0 representing a situation where all data is in one category. The ethnoracial diversity index measures evenness of the categories "Asian", "Black", "Hispanic", "White" and "Other" within the population of each census tract. The income diversity index measuers evenness of household income in categories "$19,999 and below", "$20,000 - $39,999", "$40,000 - $74,999", "$75,000 - $124,999", "$125,000 and above" within the population of each census tract. Finally, a composite diversity index was calculated as the mean of the values of the ethnoracial and normalized income diversity indices.

The unit of entropy is commonly called shannon if b=2, nat if b=e, or hartley if b=10. To simplify things considerably, it was decided to use the number of categories as the base of the logarithm (b=5 for both indices). This yields a result between 0 and 1, simplifying comparison and the calculation of a composite index considerably.

Normalizing the data in order to build the composite index proved challenging, especially since the income index is heavily "left-skewed" (meaning that the mass of the distribution is concentrated on the right side of frequency plot).
